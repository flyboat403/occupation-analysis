Description
Assistive technologists work to improve access to learning or/and improving independence
and participation for individuals with disabilities. They do this through learner support and
staff support with activities such as assessments, training and guidance. Assistive
technologists have a good understanding of learners’ needs and a wide technology
knowledge relevant to learning, living or work context. The role requires knowledge of
assistive technology hardware and software such as text to speech, prediction, dictation,
vision and physical access tools.

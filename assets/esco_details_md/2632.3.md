Description
Behavioural scientists research, observe and describe human behaviour in society. They
draw conclusions on the motives that stir actions in humans, observe the various
circumstances for different behaviours, and describe different personalities. They advise
organisations and governmental institutions on this field. They may also analyse the
behaviour of animals.

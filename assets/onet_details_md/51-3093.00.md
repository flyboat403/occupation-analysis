# 51-3093.00 - Food Cooking Machine Operators and Tenders

**Quick Facts:**
- SOC Code: 51-3093.00
- Also called: Cooker Operator, Food Production Worker, Fryer Operator, Kettle Fry Cook Operator, Machine Operator, Mogul Operator, Oven Operator, Peeler Operator, Retort Operator, Thermo Processor

## Summary

Operate or tend cooking equipment, such as steam cooking vats, deep fry cookers, pressure cookers, kettles, and boilers, to prepare food products.

## Tasks

| Importance | Category | Task |
|------------|----------|------|
| 91 | Core | Clean, wash, and sterilize equipment and cooking area, using water hoses, cleaning or sterilizing solutions, or rinses. |
| 90 | Core | Read work orders, recipes, or formulas to determine cooking times and temperatures, and ingredient specifications. |
| 89 | Core | Observe gauges, dials, and product characteristics, and adjust controls to maintain appropriate temperature, pressure, and flow of ingredients. |
| 88 | Core | Measure or weigh ingredients, using scales or measuring containers. |
| 87 | Core | Tend or operate and control equipment, such as kettles, cookers, vats and tanks, and boilers, to cook ingredients or prepare products for further processing. |
| 86 | Core | Record production and test data, such as processing steps, temperature and steam readings, cooking time, batches processed, and test results. |
| 85 | Core | Set temperature, pressure, and time controls, and start conveyers, machines, or pumps. |
| 84 | Core | Remove cooked material or products from equipment. |
| 84 | Core | Collect and examine product samples during production to test them for quality, color, content, consistency, viscosity, acidity, or specific gravity. |
| 83 | Core | Pour, dump, or load prescribed quantities of ingredients or products into cooking equipment, manually or using a hoist. |
| 82 | Core | Listen for malfunction alarms, and shut down equipment and notify supervisors when necessary. |
| 81 | Core | Notify or signal other workers to operate equipment or when processing is complete. |
| 81 | Core | Turn valves or start pumps to add ingredients or drain products from equipment and to transfer products for storage, cooling, or further processing. |
| 80 | Core | Admit required amounts of water, steam, cooking oils, or compressed air into equipment, such as by opening water valves to cool mixtures to the desired consistency. |
| 85 | Supplemental | Activate agitators and paddles to mix or stir ingredients, stopping machines when ingredients are thoroughly mixed. |
| 83 | Supplemental | Operate auxiliary machines and equipment, such as grinders, canners, and molding presses, to prepare or further process products. |
| 81 | Supplemental | Place products on conveyors or carts, and monitor product flow. |

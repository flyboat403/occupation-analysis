# 29-1299.02 - Orthoptists

**Quick Facts:**
- SOC Code: 29-1299.02
- Also called: Certified Orthoptist, Clinical Orthoptist (CO), Orthoptist

## Summary

Diagnose and treat visual system disorders such as binocular vision and eye movement impairments.

## Tasks

| Importance | Category | Task |
|------------|----------|------|
| 99 | Core | Examine patients with problems related to ocular motility, binocular vision, amblyopia, or strabismus. |
| 99 | Core | Evaluate, diagnose, or treat disorders of the visual system with an emphasis on binocular vision or abnormal eye movements. |
| 99 | Core | Provide instructions to patients or family members concerning diagnoses or treatment plans. |
| 98 | Core | Perform diagnostic tests or measurements, such as motor testing, visual acuity testing, lensometry, retinoscopy, and color vision testing. |
| 97 | Core | Provide nonsurgical interventions, including corrective lenses, patches, drops, fusion exercises, or stereograms, to treat conditions such as strabismus, heterophoria, and convergence insufficiency. |
| 96 | Core | Develop nonsurgical treatment plans for patients with conditions such as strabismus, nystagmus, and other visual disorders. |
| 93 | Core | Interpret clinical or diagnostic test results. |
| 90 | Core | Develop or use special test and communication techniques to facilitate diagnosis and treatment of children or patients with disabilities. |
| 80 | Core | Provide training related to clinical methods or orthoptics to students, resident physicians, or other health professionals. |
| 76 | Core | Refer patients to ophthalmic surgeons or other physicians. |
| 76 | Core | Prepare diagnostic or treatment reports for other medical practitioners or therapists. |
| 72 | Core | Collaborate with ophthalmologists, optometrists, or other specialists in the diagnosis, treatment, or management of conditions such as glaucoma, cataracts, and retinal diseases. |
| 63 | Core | Perform vision screening of children in schools or community health centers. |
| 63 | Core | Present or publish scientific papers. |
| 58 | Core | Participate in clinical research projects. |
| 53 | Core | Assist ophthalmologists in diagnostic ophthalmic procedures, such as ultrasonography, fundus photography, and tonometry. |

# 51-8012.00 - Power Distributors and Dispatchers

**Quick Facts:**
- SOC Code: 51-8012.00
- Also called: Control Area Operator, Control Operator, Dispatcher, Distribution System Dispatcher (DSD), DSO (Distribution System Operator), Electric System Operator, Power System Dispatcher, Power System Operator, Systems Operator, Transmission System Operator (TSO)

## Summary

Coordinate, regulate, or distribute electricity or steam.

## Tasks

| Importance | Category | Task |
|------------|----------|------|
| 94 | Core | Coordinate with engineers, planners, field personnel, or other utility workers to provide information such as clearances, switching orders, or distribution process changes. |
| 93 | Core | Respond to emergencies, such as transformer or transmission line failures, and route current around affected areas. |
| 93 | Core | Control, monitor, or operate equipment that regulates or distributes electricity or steam, using data obtained from instruments or computers. |
| 92 | Core | Direct personnel engaged in controlling or operating distribution equipment or machinery, such as instructing control room operators to start boilers or generators. |
| 92 | Core | Distribute or regulate the flow of power between entities, such as generating stations, substations, distribution lines, or users, keeping track of the status of circuits or connections. |
| 91 | Core | Manipulate controls to adjust or activate power distribution equipment or machines. |
| 91 | Core | Prepare switching orders that will isolate work areas without causing power outages, referring to drawings of power systems. |
| 89 | Core | Monitor and record switchboard or control board readings to ensure that electrical or steam distribution equipment is operating properly. |
| 83 | Core | Implement energy schedules, including real-time transmission reservations or schedules. |
| 83 | Core | Calculate load estimates or equipment requirements to determine required control settings. |
| 79 | Core | Track conditions that could affect power needs, such as changes in the weather, and adjust equipment to meet any anticipated changes. |
| 72 | Core | Record and compile operational data, such as chart or meter readings, power demands, or usage and operating times, using transmission system maps. |
| 80 | Supplemental | Inspect equipment to ensure that specifications are met or to detect any defects. |
| 78 | Supplemental | Tend auxiliary equipment used in the power distribution process. |
